Module 6

Planning and Co-Teaching Integrated Speaking and Listening Lessons

A Six-Module Professional Learning Package by Miss Mprah Ruhaimatu

1. Module Overview

This module equips student teachers with the practical ability to design, deliver, and reflect on integrated speaking and listening lessons. It covers lesson planning, lesson stages, co-teaching, and reflective teaching. It is based on the sixth unit in your course outline.

2. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, student teachers should be able to:

  1. Explain the structure of a speaking and listening lesson plan.
  2. Design integrated oral language lessons.
  3. Co-teach speaking and listening lessons effectively.
  4. Reflect critically on lesson delivery and improvement.

3. Module Content

Why Lesson Planning Matters

A lesson plan helps the teacher teach with purpose, manage time, sequence learning, assess effectively, and support all learners.

What is an Integrated Speaking and Listening Lesson?

An integrated lesson teaches speaking and listening together through meaningful interaction with clear learning goals.

Components of a Lesson Plan

A strong lesson plan includes: topic, objectives, prior knowledge, resources, procedures, assessment, reflection.

Lesson Stages

A speaking and listening lesson should include:

  • Pre-lesson: activate prior knowledge, introduce topic, motivate learners.
  • During lesson: guided oral tasks, modelling, interaction, feedback.
  • Post-lesson: reflection, consolidation, assessment.

Features of a Good Oral Lesson

A good oral lesson should be interactive, structured, purposeful, inclusive, supportive, and language-rich.

Co-Teaching

Co-teaching means two teachers or teacher-trainees work together to plan and teach a lesson.

Benefits: shared expertise, stronger classroom support, reflective improvement, collaborative professionalism.

Models of Co-Teaching

Examples: one teaches one supports, station teaching, parallel teaching, team teaching.

Reflecting After Teaching

After teaching, ask:

  • What worked well?
  • What challenged learners?
  • Did all learners participate?
  • What should be improved?

Common Problems in Lesson Delivery

Common problems: poor timing, teacher dominance, unclear instructions, weak learner engagement, poor questioning, insufficient scaffolding.

4. Interactive Learning Activities

  • Activity 1: Design a full integrated speaking and listening lesson plan.
  • Activity 2: Practice co-teaching a short oral lesson segment with a partner.
  • Activity 3: Peer feedback and reflection on a delivered lesson.

5. Assessment Tasks

Assignment

Design and deliver (or micro-teach) one integrated speaking and listening lesson. Submit the lesson plan and a short reflection on what worked and what could be improved.
