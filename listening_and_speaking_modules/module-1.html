Module 1

Foundations of Teaching Listening and Speaking

A Six-Module Professional Learning Package by Miss Mprah Ruhaimatu

1. Module Overview

This module introduces student teachers to the foundational concepts of speaking and listening as essential components of language learning and literacy development. It also addresses common misconceptions about oral language learning and examines the major components of effective speaking, including pronunciation, grammar, vocabulary, fluency, comprehension, and oral instruction. This module is based on the first unit of your outline.

2. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, student teachers should be able to:

  1. Define speaking and listening in educational contexts.
  2. Explain the role of speaking and listening in language learning and literacy development.
  3. Identify misconceptions about speaking and listening.
  4. Discuss the major components of speaking.
  5. Demonstrate understanding of effective oral instruction in the JHS classroom.

3. Module Content

Why Listening and Speaking Matter

  • Listening and speaking are the first language skills children develop.
  • They support: reading development, writing development, vocabulary growth, classroom participation, confidence and identity.
  • Oral language is the foundation of literacy.

Note: Learners who struggle to express themselves orally often struggle academically in other language areas.

What is Speaking?

Speaking is the productive oral language skill through which learners express ideas, emotions, opinions, and information in meaningful contexts.

Key features: articulation of sounds, organization of ideas, vocabulary use, fluency, appropriateness to audience and context.

What is Listening?

Listening is an active process of receiving, interpreting, and responding to spoken language.

Involves: hearing, attention, meaning-making, interpretation, response.

Important: Listening is not the same as silence. A learner may be quiet but not listening.

Role of Speaking and Listening in Language Learning

  • Build vocabulary
  • Develop pronunciation
  • Practice grammar naturally
  • Improve confidence
  • Understand classroom instructions
  • Collaborate socially
  • Construct meaning

Misconceptions about Speaking and Listening

  • “Good speakers are born, not trained.”
  • “Listening is passive.”
  • “Only fluent English speakers can participate.”
  • “Quiet learners are always attentive.”
  • “Speaking activities are a waste of instructional time.”

Challenge: Good oral communication can be taught and scaffolded.

Components of Speaking

  • Pronunciation
  • Grammar
  • Vocabulary
  • Fluency
  • Comprehension

Strong speaking instruction should develop all these elements together.

Pronunciation

Refers to how sounds, stress, rhythm, and intonation are produced.

Why it matters: clarity of speech, intelligibility, confidence.

Teaching examples: minimal pairs, choral repetition, tongue twisters, sound modelling.

Grammar and Vocabulary

Grammar helps organize ideas meaningfully. Vocabulary gives learners the words needed to express ideas precisely.

Teaching implications: teach grammar in context, pre-teach vocabulary, use sentence frames and language supports.

Fluency and Comprehension

Fluency = ability to speak smoothly and meaningfully.
Comprehension = ability to understand and respond appropriately.

Ways to build them: pair talk, guided discussion, storytelling, listening-response tasks, role-play.

Effective Oral Instruction

Effective oral instruction should be: interactive, learner-centred, inclusive, scaffolded, purposeful, culturally responsive.

Teachers should: model language, ask open-ended questions, create safe speaking spaces, give constructive feedback.

4. Interactive Learning Activities

  • Activity 1: Think-Pair-Share
    Prompt: “What is the difference between hearing and listening?”
  • Activity 2: Misconception Sorting
    Students work in groups to sort statements into: True, False, Needs qualification.
  • Activity 3: Component Mapping
    Each group explains one speaking component and how to teach it in a JHS classroom.

5. Assessment Tasks

Quiz Questions

  • Define speaking and listening.
  • Explain two misconceptions about speaking and listening.
  • Discuss three components of speaking.

Assignment

Write a 500-word reflection on: “Why should speaking and listening be treated as central literacy skills in JHS education?”
