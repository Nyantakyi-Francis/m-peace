Module 5

Understanding the Speaking and Listening Component of the Curriculum

A Six-Module Professional Learning Package by Miss Mprah Ruhaimatu

1. Module Overview

This module helps student teachers understand how speaking and listening are represented in the curriculum and how to interpret curriculum expectations into teachable classroom plans. It also introduces student teachers to critical curriculum review. It is based on the fifth unit of your outline.

2. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, student teachers should be able to:

  1. Interpret the speaking and listening components of the English curriculum.
  2. Identify strengths and gaps in curriculum design.
  3. Translate curriculum expectations into a scheme of work.
  4. Evaluate curriculum responsiveness to diverse learners.

3. Module Content

What is Curriculum Interpretation?

Curriculum interpretation means understanding what the curriculum requires, translating curriculum intentions into classroom teaching, and aligning content, methods, and assessment.

Why Teachers Must Understand Curriculum

Teachers who understand curriculum can teach purposefully, sequence lessons effectively, assess appropriately, and avoid random teaching.

Key Questions for Curriculum Reading

When reading a speaking and listening curriculum, ask:

  • What should learners know?
  • What should learners be able to do?
  • How should learning be assessed?
  • How inclusive is the content?

Speaking and Listening in the Curriculum

A good curriculum should include oral expression, listening comprehension, oral interaction, pronunciation practice, discussion and dialogue, and communication for real-life purposes.

Deficiencies That May Exist

Possible curriculum deficiencies: unclear progression, weak assessment guidance, insufficient oral practice tasks, poor attention to multilingual learners, limited technology integration.

Evaluating Curriculum Inclusiveness

Ask:

  • Does the curriculum support shy learners?
  • Does it support multilingual learners?
  • Does it provide varied oral tasks?
  • Does it allow participation for all?

From Curriculum to Scheme of Work

A scheme of work should include: week/topic, objectives, content, activities, resources, assessment.

Principles of a Good Scheme of Work

A good scheme of work should be realistic, sequential, learner-centred, measurable, and curriculum-aligned.

Teacher as Curriculum Interpreter

The teacher is not just a curriculum follower. The teacher is a curriculum decision-maker.

Reflective Question

“When curriculum is weak or unclear, what should a competent teacher do?”

4. Interactive Learning Activities

  • Activity 1: Curriculum Critique
    Students analyze a curriculum excerpt and identify strengths, gaps, and teaching implications.
  • Activity 2: Scheme of Work Drafting
    Students prepare a 2-week scheme of work on speaking and listening.
  • Activity 3: Inclusive Curriculum Reflection
    Students discuss how curriculum can exclude certain learners unintentionally.

5. Assessment Tasks

Assignment

Prepare a mini scheme of work for one speaking and listening unit and explain how it responds to learner diversity.
