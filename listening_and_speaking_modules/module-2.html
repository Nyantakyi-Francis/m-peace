Module 2

Strategies for Teaching Listening and Speaking

A Six-Module Professional Learning Package by Miss Mprah Ruhaimatu

1. Module Overview

This module equips student teachers with practical classroom strategies for teaching listening and speaking. It focuses on learner-centred oral activities such as storytelling, questioning, debates, group discussion, description, modelling, audio-visual resources, and listening comprehension. It aligns with the second unit in your outline.

2. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, student teachers should be able to:

  1. Identify effective strategies for teaching speaking and listening.
  2. Evaluate the strengths and limitations of each strategy.
  3. Apply suitable oral language strategies to diverse learner contexts.
  4. Design classroom activities using speaking and listening techniques.

3. Module Content

Why Teaching Strategies Matter

A teaching strategy is a structured method used to support learner understanding and participation.

Good oral language strategies should promote interaction, support inclusion, engage shy learners, build confidence, and support comprehension.

Storytelling

Storytelling helps learners sequence ideas, improve vocabulary, build imagination, strengthen fluency, and practice listening attentively.

Classroom uses: personal storytelling, retelling folktales, picture-based storytelling.

Open-Ended Questioning

Open-ended questions encourage critical thinking, extended speaking, reasoning, and interpretation.

Examples: “Why do you think the character acted that way?”, “What would you do differently?”, “How would you describe this place?”

Group Discussion and Debate

These strategies help learners express opinions, defend ideas, listen respectfully, build confidence, and negotiate meaning.

Teacher role: set clear rules, scaffold participation, manage turn-taking.

Reinforcing Active Listening

Active listening includes eye contact, attention, note-taking, asking follow-up questions, and responding meaningfully.

Teaching methods: listen-and-draw, listen-and-act, listen-and-retell.

Description Activities

Students describe people, objects, places, events, or feelings.

Benefits: vocabulary expansion, detail awareness, speaking confidence.

Use of Audio and Audio-Visual Materials

Examples: podcasts, recorded dialogues, songs, short videos, classroom recordings.

Benefits: authentic pronunciation, real-world listening, engagement, exposure to multiple voices.

Modelling Speaking and Listening

The teacher must model how to respond, how to listen, how to ask questions, how to pronounce words, and how to participate respectfully.

Important principle: Do not ask learners to do what you have not demonstrated.

Listening Comprehension

Listening comprehension involves understanding spoken texts, identifying key ideas, responding accurately, and inferring meaning.

Activities: listen and answer, sequencing tasks, note completion, gist and detail listening.

Problems in Using These Strategies

Challenges may include large class size, learner shyness, language diversity, limited materials, time constraints, and poor classroom management.

Note: Strategies are powerful, but they must be adapted to context.

4. Interactive Learning Activities

  • Activity 1: Microteaching Rotation
    Each group teaches one strategy in 5 minutes.
  • Activity 2: School Observation Task
    Students observe how oral language is taught in a school and report: strategy used, strengths, weaknesses, recommendations.
  • Activity 3: Strategy Match
    Students match: classroom problem → best oral strategy.

5. Assessment Tasks

Quiz Questions

  • Explain the difference between storytelling and discussion as oral teaching strategies.
  • Why are open-ended questions important in speaking lessons?
  • State three challenges in teaching speaking and listening.

Assignment

Prepare a 15-minute speaking/listening activity for JHS and justify your choice of strategy.
