Module 3

Technology and the Development of Speaking and Listening Materials

A Six-Module Professional Learning Package by Miss Mprah Ruhaimatu

1. Module Overview

This module introduces student teachers to the role of technology in designing and using instructional materials for speaking and listening lessons. It explores technology-based teaching-learning materials (TLMs), practical classroom applications, and the challenges teachers may face in digital integration. This is drawn from the third unit of your outline.

2. Learning Outcomes

By the end of this module, student teachers should be able to:

  1. Define speaking and listening TLMs.
  2. Identify digital tools for teaching oral language.
  3. Create simple technology-based speaking and listening materials.
  4. Evaluate the challenges of technology integration.

3. Module Content

What are TLMs?

TLMs = Teaching and Learning Materials

For speaking and listening, they may include: audio recordings, videos, flashcards, podcasts, dialogue scripts, speech samples, listening worksheets, digital slides.

Why Technology Matters

Technology can increase engagement, provide authentic listening input, support pronunciation practice, allow repetition and replay, and promote learner independence.

Technology Tools for Speaking and Listening

Examples: smartphones, laptops, projectors, voice recorders, YouTube clips, podcasts, language apps, Google Slides / PowerPoint, WhatsApp voice notes, LMS discussion boards.

Technology for Teaching Speaking

Teachers can use technology to record student speeches, model pronunciation, assign oral presentations, support peer feedback, and provide oral rehearsal opportunities.

Technology for Teaching Listening

Teachers can use technology to play authentic listening passages, pause and replay difficult sections, expose learners to different accents, and teach listening comprehension.

Designing Digital TLMs

Good digital materials should be age appropriate, culturally relevant, easy to use, clear in purpose, and accessible for diverse learners.

Examples of TLMs Student Teachers Can Create

  • Audio vocabulary bank
  • Pronunciation practice slides
  • Short dialogue recordings
  • Comprehension questions from video/audio
  • Role-play cue cards in digital format

Challenges of Using Technology

Common challenges: poor internet access, limited devices, unstable electricity, teacher confidence gaps, learner distraction, digital inequality.

Solutions to Technology Challenges

Teachers can download materials offline, use low-tech options, share devices in groups, use mobile-friendly tools, and combine digital and non-digital methods.

Best Practice Principle

Technology should not be used because it is available. It should be used because it improves learning.

4. Interactive Learning Activities

  • Activity 1: Tool Hunt
    Groups list 10 tools they can use for oral language teaching in low-resource and high-resource contexts.
  • Activity 2: Material Design Challenge
    Students create one digital speaking or listening TLM.
  • Activity 3: Tech Debate
    Topic: “Technology improves oral language learning more than traditional methods.”

5. Assessment Tasks

Assignment

Design one technology-supported speaking or listening lesson material and explain: target learners, objective, use in class, adaptation for low-resource settings.
